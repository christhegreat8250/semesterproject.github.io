John F. Kennedy

Biography

John Fitzgerald Kennedy, often referred to as JFK, was the 35th President of the United States. Born on May 29, 1917, in Brookline, Massachusetts, he became one of the most iconic figures in American history.

Legacy

John F. Kennedy's legacy includes initiatives such as the Peace Corps, the space program, and his famous inaugural address emphasizing civic duty. His tragic assassination on November 22, 1963, in Dallas, Texas, left a lasting impact on the nation.

Quotes

"Ask not what your country can do for you, ask what you can do for your country." - John F. Kennedy
